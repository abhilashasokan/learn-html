Intranet

An intranet is a network that exists exclusively within an organization. Intranet is developed by using internet technology to deliver internal information over the local area network accessible only to employees or other invited users. It is used not only for transferring documents within an organization but also for linking software, databases and hardware into universal network. Intranet in firewalls and is not accessible to outsiders. File sharing between geographically groups of regional offices is one of the major uses of It is used in posting company policy and information, Job announcements and conducting internal surveys. Intranet is widely used by educational institutions and business irrespective of their size, for their internal communication purpose. The intranets are used within the organization for following purposes:

  1. Post personal web pages.
  2. Access product, manuals, contracts, customer data etc.
  3. Post memos, circulars, newsletter etc.
  4. Revision, validation, approval of documents.
  5. Access central databases.
  6. Conducting employee development programs.
  7. Access schedules, plan, calendars, employee database etc.

Sales and marketing intranets, Research and Development intranets, customer service and technical support intranet, human resource intranet, accounting intranet etc., are the different specific purpose intranet applications.

Extranet

An extranet can be viewed as part of a company's intranet that is extended to users outside the company, usually the Internet. Intranet is private to the organization where as Extranet is an Intranet for outside authorized users. The outside users are trusted partners of the organization who have access to information of their interest and concern. For example, Dealers or distributors have access to product files such as catalogues, product specifications, images etc., to answer queries of the customers.

In short, an extranet is a private network that uses Internet protocols, network connectivity, and possibly the public telecommunication system to securely share part of an organization's information or operations with suppliers, vendors, partners, customers or other businesses.

An extranet can offer a range of benefits to business from lowering costs, to producing faster results and improving the quality of service to customers. The various advantages of extranet are as follows;

  1. It can be used to share product catalogs exclusively with trade partners.
  2. It supports collaboration with other companies on joint development efforts.
  3. It helps to jointly develop and use training programs with other companies.
  4. Extranet provides or access services provided by one company to a group of other companies, such as an online banking application managed by one company on behalf of affiliated banks.
  5. It can be used to share news of common interest exclusively.
